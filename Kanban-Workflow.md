**How does a Kanban board work, and how does it help manage workflow?**
A Kanban board shows all tasks in one place. Tasks move from left to right as work progresses. It helps teams see priorities, track progress, and stay organized.

**What do the different columns on a Kanban board represent? (e.g., Backlog, In Progress, Blocked, Done)**
Backlog / Not Started: Tasks that are planned but not started yet
In Progress: Tasks the team is currently working on
Blocked: Tasks that cannot continue due to issues
Done / Completed: Tasks that are finished and approved

**How do tasks move through the board, and who is responsible for updating them?**
Team members move their tasks from column to column as work progresses. Everyone is responsible for updating their own tasks regularly.

**What are the benefits of limiting work in progress (WIP)?**
Prevents overload
Helps focus on fewer tasks at a time
Improves quality and speed
Makes workflow easier to manage
