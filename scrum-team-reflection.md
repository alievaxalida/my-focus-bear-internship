🏆 Milestone: Working in a Scrum Team
🤝 How to Be a Member of a Scrum Team
🎯 Goal
Understand how QA fits into Agile Scrum teams and what responsibilities testers have in a sprint.

❓ Why is this important?
Scrum teams rely on collaboration and fast feedback to deliver high-quality software. QA testers play a key role in validating features, catching issues early, and ensuring smooth releases. Understanding Scrum roles, ceremonies, and workflows helps QA work efficiently within the team.

🔍 Research & Learn
**What are the key roles in a Scrum team, and where does QA fit in?**
The main roles are:
Product Owner – decides priorities and manages the backlog.
Scrum Master – supports the team and removes blockers.
Developers – build the product.
QA (Tester) – checks quality and tests features.
QA is part of the Scrum Team and works closely with developers to ensure quality.
**What happens in a Sprint Planning, Daily Standup, Sprint Review, and Retrospective?**
Sprint Planning: The team selects tasks for the sprint and plans the work.
Daily Standup: Short meeting to share progress and blockers.
Sprint Review: The team shows completed work and gets feedback.
Retrospective: The team discusses improvements for the next sprint.
**How does QA help the team maintain quality throughout the sprint?**
QA tests features early, reports bugs quickly, checks acceptance criteria, and communicates with developers to fix issues fast.
**What is shift-left testing, and why is early involvement of QA important?**
Shift-left testing means testing starts early in the development process.
Early QA involvement helps find problems sooner, reduce last-minute stress, and improve quality.
**How do testers collaborate with developers, product managers, and designers in Scrum?**
QA clarifies requirements with the Product Owner, discusses issues with developers, and checks design expectations with designers to ensure everything works correctly.

📝 Reflection
**How should QA prepare for a Sprint Planning meeting?**
QA should review backlog items, understand requirements, think about risks, and prepare test ideas.
**What information should testers share in a Daily Standup?**
What was tested, what will be tested next, and any blockers or issues.
**If a feature is about to be released, but QA finds a critical bug, what should happen next?**
The team should stop the release, fix the bug, retest the feature, and confirm stability before releasing.
