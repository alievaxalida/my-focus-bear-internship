**Why do QA bottlenecks happen in Agile teams?**
QA bottlenecks happen when testing starts too late, many features are ready at the same time, or requirements are unclear. Sometimes QA has too many tasks and not enough time.
**How can testers avoid testing everything at the last minute?**
Testers can start testing early, test small parts step by step, and prepare test cases before development is finished.
**What is parallel testing, and how does it help keep QA on track?**
Parallel testing means QA tests features while developers are still building other features. This helps find bugs earlier and prevents too much testing at the end of the sprint.
**How do developers and testers collaborate to ensure smooth testing progress?**
They communicate daily, clarify requirements together, share progress in stand-ups, and fix bugs quickly. Early communication reduces delays.
**What techniques can help QA work more efficiently without compromising quality?**
Prioritizing critical features, reusing test cases, clear bug reports, and focusing on high-risk areas help QA work faster and better.

**How can early involvement of QA prevent last-minute testing rushes?**
Early involvement helps QA understand requirements, prepare tests early, and catch issues before development is finished.
